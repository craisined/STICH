A machine-learning project

Turn humming into classical music—and back.

STICH is an AI that translates between two musical worlds: the tune you hum and a richer classical rendition of it. Hum a melody, hear it reimagined. Play a classical phrase, hear how someone might hum it.

Hear examples Try it yourself

What is it?

Think of a translator — but for music.

When you translate a sentence from English to Spanish, the meaning stays the same but the words change. STICH does the same thing with music. It takes a simple hummed melody and re-voices it in a classical style — and it can go the other direction too.

The clever part: nobody handed it matched examples. We never showed it "this exact hum equals this exact classical piece." It learned the style of each world on its own and figured out how to travel between them.

How it works

Two artists and two critics, learning by competing.

🎨

The artists

Two AI "composers." One turns humming into classical; the other turns classical into humming.

🧐

The critics

Two AI "judges" try to spot fakes. If a judge can't tell the result from the real thing, the artist is doing well.

🔁

The safety check

Cycle-consistency: translate a tune and translate it back — you should get close to the original. That keeps the melody intact.

In machine-learning terms this is a CycleGAN: two generators, two discriminators, trained with adversarial and cycle-consistency losses.

Under the hood

For the curious.

Model
CycleGAN — 2 generators + 2 discriminators, 1-D convolutions with ResNet blocks over raw audio.
Humming data
HumTrans — thousands of recorded hummed melodies.
Classical data
MusicNet — hundreds of classical recordings.
Audio format
Mono, 16 kHz, processed into 10-second clips.
Training
PyTorch, multi-GPU (DDP) with mixed precision.
This site
Plain HTML, CSS & JavaScript, with a Flask server for the model.
